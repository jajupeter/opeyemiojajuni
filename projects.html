Immersive Crisis Leader Emergency Response Training (ICLERT) Applied research Project

Project Overview

ICLERT is a university–agency collaboration applied research project that redefines how crisis leadership is taught and practiced. Born in the aftermath of COVID-19, when the demand for scalable and adaptable training became urgent, ICLERT combines academic research expertise with public health and emergency management partners, leveraging high-fidelity VR and digital twin technology to replace static tabletop drills with hyper-realistic, scenario-driven simulations. From hurricanes to electrical failures and gas leaks, the platform immerses emergency managers, police, and risk officers in complex, concurrent incidents where split-second decisions can be practiced safely, without operational risk. The outcome of the project is a scalable, replicable model that not only advances applied research in VR/AR and data-driven training, but also elevates confidence, accelerates response times, and strengthens organizational resilience in the face of real-world crisis.

Approach

We built a LiDAR-accurate 3D digital twin of Southern University, integrating scenario-driven VR modules for hurricanes, gas leaks, and electrical failures to simulate concurrent crises under pressure. A modular curriculum (3 foundational, 7 hazard-specific, 2 integrated simulations) was optimized for Meta Quest 3 and CAVE systems, enabling scalable deployment. An analytics layer with assessments, group tests, and feedback dashboards generated measurable insights into learning gains, decision speed, and user confidence. All content, code, and documentation was packaged for replication, ensuring rapid adoption across universities, agencies, and statewide partners

Outcome

We have already trained 35+ crisis leaders through immersive, real-time VR scenarios, demonstrating measurable gains in decision-making speed, confidence, and crisis readiness. With 10+ deployment-ready modules now in use, the program not only strengthens frontline response but also inspires a new generation of students to pursue careers in emergency management. In partnership with the Louisiana Department of Health, ICLERT showcases the power of university–agency collaboration, delivering a replicable model that strengthens workforce resilience, supports regulatory compliance, and advances statewide disaster preparedness.
