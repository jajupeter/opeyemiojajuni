Immersive Crisis Leader Emergency Response Training (ICLERT)

ICLERT closes the gap left by traditional drills with AI‑driven VR that captures the chaos of real incidents. Leaders practice split‑second judgment in lifelike scenarios to measurably improve readiness.

We engineered a LiDAR/GIS digital twin of campus (shelter + EOC), then built a modular curriculum—10 crisis courses + 2 hazard scenarios (gas leaks, electrical failures)—for Meta Quest 3 and CAVE.

35+ leaders trained; 10+ modules deployed. In partnership with the Louisiana Department of Health, the program scales statewide to strengthen workforce resilience and compliance.

Immersive Geospatial Visualization in VR Systems

Static 2D maps limit spatial reasoning. We transform OSM/ArcGIS data into procedural 3D city scenes in CityEngine and deploy to VR/CAVE for deep, interactive exploration.

Unity deployment with local‑origin georeferencing ensures precision on Meta Quest 3 and multi‑user CAVE displays. Usability studies validated realism and value for planning, education, and engagement.

The result: a city‑agnostic framework ready for real‑time feeds (traffic, weather) to evolve into dynamic urban digital twins.

SafeGuide Campus: Generative AI for Safer Campus

Universities face rising safety and preparedness challenges, while traditional tools lack personalization and immediacy. SafeGuide Campus fills this gap with an interactive Generative AI assistant that helps students, faculty and staff navigate risky situations like intoxication, harassment, and unsafe environments.

Built in PartyRock, the platform uses large language models to simulate real-time risk scenarios, provide tailored guidance, and link people to resources. It also serves as a research sandbox for exploring responsible GenAI deployment with future VR integrations.

SafeGuide Campus demonstrates how applied AI research can drive real-world impact by improving student, faculty and staff well-being, readiness, and safety culture. It highlights scalable GenAI solutions that strengthen campus resilience and enable safer learning environments.

SURADD: Reaching Across the Digital Divide

Assessment‑driven pilots to extend broadband access, device availability, and digital skills in underserved communities.

We combined community needs analyses with training modules and partner deployments to validate sustainable, equitable access models.

Outputs include policy insights, adoption playbooks, and replicable training kits for regional scale‑up.

Evaluating User Experience of VR‑Based Learning

Cohort observational study with focus groups and in‑class observation to quantify presence, usability, workload, and satisfaction in VR learning.

We triangulated surveys, behavioral metrics, and interviews to link UX factors to learning outcomes.

Findings inform headset setup, interaction patterns, and instructional design for higher impact VR courses.

Immersive VR Learning: Cognitive Skill Impact

Randomized, controlled exposure to VR learning modules with pre/post testing using validated CT instruments.

Measured gains in algorithmic thinking, creativity, and problem solving; analyzed effect sizes and subgroup differences.

Results guide where VR delivers the strongest cognitive returns and how to structure sessions for maximal gain.

Predicting Computational Thinking with ML

Built ML classifiers on survey items and performance tasks to predict CT proficiency levels.

Feature importance surfaced algorithmic and creative thinking as dominant predictors; ablation studies confirmed robustness.

Delivers early‑signal analytics to personalize instruction and target support.

Validating a CT Survey with EFA, PCA & SEM

Dimensionality reduction trimmed 29 observed variables to 5 latent constructs using EFA/PCA; SEM confirmed factor structure and fit.

Reliability and validity established for VR‑learning contexts with cross‑validation across cohorts.

Provides a rigorous instrument for future CT studies and program evaluation.

Cloud‑Based STEM Success Prediction

Deployed a privacy‑aware web app that ingests academic/behavioral signals to forecast student outcomes and risk levels.

MLOps pipeline supports model retraining, monitoring, and explainability for advisors and program leads.

Improves early intervention decisions and program resource allocation.

Forecasting Workforce Diversity in Construction (ARIMA)

Time‑series analyses of graduation pipelines vs. industry demand to project minority workforce supply.

Model diagnostics, scenario testing, and confidence intervals inform recruitment and scholarship strategies.

Actionable forecasts for universities and employers to close representation gaps.

Benchmarking ML for Drunk‑Driving Detection

Compared classical and deep models on multi‑sensor time‑series for impairment detection under latency constraints.

Reported ROC‑AUC, precision/recall, and drift robustness; identified optimal models per deployment context.

Informs on‑device vs. edge inference choices for real‑time road safety systems.
